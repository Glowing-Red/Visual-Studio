Jurassic Park

What is Jurassic Park about?

Jurassic Park is a thrilling science fiction film where a billionaire creates a dinosaur theme park. As visitors explore, the park's security systems fail, unleashing chaos and danger as scientists and guests must confront the resurrected prehistoric creatures in a fight for survival.

Release

Jurassic Park was released on June 11, 1993. The film was directed by the renowned filmmaker Steven Spielberg and is based on the 1990 novel of the same name by Michael Crichton. It became a groundbreaking science fiction adventure and a cultural phenomenon, known for its revolutionary use of computer-generated imagery (CGI) to bring dinosaurs to life on the big screen. The movie's success led to the creation of a highly successful franchise, including sequels, merchandise, and theme park attractions.
